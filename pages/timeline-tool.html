Record Your Timeline

Document each event in your housing dispute. This timeline can be used as evidence.

How to Use This Tool

  • Record events in chronological order as they happen
  • Be specific and factual in your descriptions
  • Include exact dates and times when possible
  • Document all communications (emails, calls, letters)
  • Note responses or lack of response from your landlord
  • Export your timeline and keep copies
  • Your timeline is saved locally - it won't be lost if you close the page

Timeline Tips for Disputes

What to Record:

  • Date of repair request and method (email, phone, letter)
  • Date of follow-up communications
  • Date repairs were finally completed (or not)
  • Any verbal conversations (with date and what was said)
  • Dates photographs were taken of issues
  • Any agreements made or promises broken
  • Visits by landlord or contractors and what happened
  • Impact on you (e.g., "no heating from X to Y date")

Example Entry:

  • ❌ "Landlord ignored repair request" (too vague)
  • ✓ "15 Jan 2024 - Sent email to landlord reporting broken heating system, no response received"

Using Your Timeline as Evidence

  • Print and keep a copy for your records
  • Use it to support complaints to local council
  • Share it with Citizens Advice or Shelter advisors
  • Present it as evidence if pursuing legal action
  • Use it to back up claims about impact on you

Privacy & Data

Your timeline is saved only on your device using browser storage. It is not sent to our servers or stored anywhere else. Only you can access it.
