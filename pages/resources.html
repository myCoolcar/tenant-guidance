Legal Resources for Tenants

Below are key pieces of UK legislation, regulations, and important case law that define tenant rights and landlord responsibilities. Each resource links directly to the official legislation page.

Housing Act 1988

View the Housing Act 1988

The Housing Act 1988 established the modern private rental system in England, including the creation of assured and assured shorthold tenancies. It sets out many of the legal rules around possession, rent, and tenancy agreements. Most private tenants in England today hold an Assured Shorthold Tenancy created under this Act.

Landlord and Tenant Act 1985

View the Landlord and Tenant Act 1985

This Act establishes key landlord obligations, particularly regarding repairs and maintenance. Section 11 requires landlords to maintain the structure of the property and ensure that heating, water, gas, and sanitation systems are kept in working order. Many repair disputes rely on this legislation.

Tenant Fees Act 2019

View the Tenant Fees Act 2019

The Tenant Fees Act 2019 banned most letting agent and landlord fees charged to tenants in England. Only a small number of payments are allowed, such as rent, deposits, and certain default fees. Any prohibited fees can normally be challenged and reclaimed.

Housing Act 2004

View the Housing Act 2004

The Housing Act 2004 introduced deposit protection schemes and the Housing Health and Safety Rating System (HHSRS). This law allows local councils to inspect housing conditions and enforce improvements where properties present health risks.

Protection from Eviction Act 1977

View the Protection from Eviction Act 1977

This Act makes it a criminal offence for landlords to unlawfully evict tenants or harass them into leaving a property. In most cases, landlords must follow formal legal processes through the courts before removing tenants.

Deregulation Act 2015

View the Deregulation Act 2015

The Deregulation Act introduced new protections against retaliatory eviction. If tenants report serious repair problems, landlords may be restricted from using Section 21 eviction notices shortly afterwards.

Spencer v Taylor (2013) – Court of Appeal

View the case on BAILII

This case clarified how Section 21 eviction notices should be interpreted when tenancies become periodic after a fixed term. It significantly affected how landlords serve possession notices under the Housing Act 1988.

Street v Mountford (1985) – House of Lords

View the case on BAILII

This landmark case established the legal definition of a tenancy versus a licence. It confirmed that if a tenant has exclusive possession of a property for a term and pays rent, the arrangement is usually a tenancy regardless of what the contract is called.

Deposit Protection Schemes

  • Deposit Protection Service (DPS)

    The DPS is one of the government-approved tenancy deposit protection schemes. Landlords must protect tenant deposits in an authorised scheme and provide the tenant with prescribed information.

  • MyDeposits Scheme

    MyDeposits is another authorised scheme for protecting tenancy deposits. Tenants can raise disputes through the scheme if they disagree with deductions made at the end of a tenancy.

  • Tenancy Deposit Scheme (TDS)

    The TDS provides deposit protection and dispute resolution services. Tenants can challenge unfair deductions through the scheme’s adjudication process.

Housing Standards and Safety

Housing Health and Safety Rating System (HHSRS)

The HHSRS is the system used by local authorities to assess housing conditions and hazards. Councils can order landlords to make improvements if serious health risks such as damp, mould, or fire hazards are identified.
