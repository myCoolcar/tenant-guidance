💷 Tenant Fees Act 2019

Letting fees.

Most fees charged by landlords and letting agents are illegal. Here is exactly what you can be charged — and everything you can refuse.

✅

The Only Permitted Payments

Everything else is banned by the Tenant Fees Act 2019

Since 1 June 2019, landlords and letting agents in England can only require tenants to make the following payments. That is the complete list — nothing has been left out.

🏠

Rent

Your periodic rent as set out in the tenancy agreement.

🔒

Security Deposit

Capped at 5 weeks' rent where annual rent is under £50,000, or 6 weeks where it is £50,000 or above. Must be protected.

📋

Holding Deposit

To reserve a property only. Capped at one week's rent. Must be repaid or credited against rent when you move in.

There are also two narrow default payments a landlord may charge: late payment interest on rent more than 14 days overdue (capped at 3% above the Bank of England base rate), and replacement of a lost key or security device at the actual cost incurred, supported by evidence.

Legal basis: Tenant Fees Act 2019, Schedule 1. This Act applies to all assured shorthold tenancies in England from 1 June 2019.
🚫

Fees You Should Never Pay

The following charges are unlawful under the Tenant Fees Act 2019. If you have already paid any of them, you are entitled to a refund.

✗

Application or administration fees

✗

Referencing or credit check fees

✗

Guarantor arrangement fees

✗

Inventory fees (check-in or check-out)

✗

✗

✗

✗

✗

✗

Viewings fees

⚠️

Penalties for Landlords Who Break the Rules

Trading Standards enforces the Tenant Fees Act 2019. The penalties are significant.

£5,000
Maximum civil penalty for a first breach of the Tenant Fees Act 2019
£30,000
Maximum penalty for a further breach within five years — or a criminal prosecution and banning order

Additionally, a landlord or agent who has taken a prohibited payment cannot lawfully serve a valid Section 21 notice until the money has been repaid.

📝

What to Do if You Are Asked for an Illegal Fee

1

Refuse to pay. You have no legal obligation to pay a prohibited fee. Do not feel pressured.

2

Request a refund in writing if you have already paid. Email is best. State that the charge is prohibited under the Tenant Fees Act 2019 and request repayment within 14 days.

3

Report to your local council. Trading Standards is responsible for enforcing the Act. Contact your local council to make a formal complaint. You can do this even if you ultimately paid the fee.

4

Seek advice or pursue a court claim. If the fee is not refunded, you can seek a County Court judgment to recover it. Contact Citizens Advice or Shelter for guidance before starting a claim.

📌

Holding Deposits Explained

A holding deposit is the only pre-tenancy payment (other than rent) that is permitted, and it comes with strict rules.

1

It must not exceed one week's rent.

2

The landlord or agent must refund it — or put it towards your first rent or deposit — within 15 calendar days of the tenancy starting.

3

The landlord may keep it only in specific circumstances: if you withdraw from the tenancy, fail a right to rent check, provide false or misleading information, or fail to enter the tenancy agreement within the 15-day deadline through your own fault.

4

If the landlord withdraws from the tenancy or fails to progress it without a permitted reason, the holding deposit must be returned to you in full.

🔍

Red Flags When Viewing a Property

Be cautious of letting agents or landlords who do any of the following — each one may indicate they are not complying with the law.

Ask for any upfront payment before you have signed a tenancy agreement, beyond a holding deposit

Refer to "admin fees", "referencing fees", or "application fees" as though they are routine

Refuse to provide a written breakdown of all charges before you commit

Request cash payment with no receipt

Pressure you to pay quickly to "secure the property" beyond a lawful holding deposit

Charged an illegal fee? Generate a formal letter requesting a refund, citing the Tenant Fees Act 2019.
