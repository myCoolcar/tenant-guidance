Deposit Protection Requirements

All tenancy deposits must be protected in a government-approved scheme. Your landlord or letting agent must:

  • Register your deposit with an approved scheme within 30 days of receiving it
  • Provide you with prescribed information about the scheme in writing
  • Keep your deposit in a designated account separate from their own money
  • Return your full deposit at the end of the tenancy (minus any agreed deductions)

The Three Approved Schemes

  • The Deposit Protection Service (TDS)
  • MyDeposits
  • Deposit Protection (DPS)

Permitted Deductions

Your landlord can only deduct from your deposit for:

  • Rent arrears (unpaid rent)
  • Damage beyond normal wear and tear
  • Cleaning costs (only if this was specified in your tenancy agreement)
  • Replacement of missing items (e.g., keys)

Important: Normal wear and tear is NOT deductible. Your landlord cannot charge for items that have naturally worn out through ordinary use.

What Is Normal Wear and Tear?

  • Faded paint and wallpaper
  • Worn carpet and flooring
  • Minor marks on walls
  • Worn light fixtures
  • Natural aging of appliances

These items CANNOT be charged to you unless they are damaged beyond normal use.

Disputing Deductions

If you disagree with deductions made from your deposit, you have the right to dispute them through the deposit protection scheme's free dispute resolution service.

  • Most schemes offer free, independent dispute resolution
  • You can submit evidence (photos, receipts, witness statements)
  • The scheme will make a binding decision
  • The scheme can order your landlord to return funds

If Your Deposit Wasn't Protected

If your landlord failed to protect your deposit properly or didn't provide prescribed information, you may be able to claim compensation:

  • Compensation of up to 3 times the deposit amount
  • You can claim through the court (County Court)
  • Seek legal advice immediately if this applies to you
  • Contact Citizens Advice or Shelter for support

Checklist When You Move Out

  • Get an inventory report at the end of your tenancy
  • Take photos/videos of the property condition
  • Get confirmation from your landlord about what will be charged
  • Get the name of the deposit protection scheme
  • Allow reasonable time for deductions to be itemized
  • If you disagree, use the scheme's dispute process
